Personal Manifesto , Altruistic, Sustainable Tech

A short, honest statement of values, hopes, and ideas about where we are as a species and where our science, technology & engineering should go.

Where I stand

I'm disappointed by where we are as a species, where we are heading. Our primal instincts are overtaking reasoning and instead of optimizing for sustainability, wellbeing, health, meaning, impact, curiosity, altruism , we are falling short to greed, consumerism, the chase for more and meaningless monkey capitalism.

My focus: STEM & technology

I'm particularly saddened by our STEM state. I think what I'm saying applies to a lot more, but this is my passion and domain. Are we in science, tech & engineering optimizing for the environment? For fellow human beings' wellbeing and health? For future generations? For curiosity, altruism and sustainability?

It seems that all we do is capitalize profits, consume more and disregard the ecosystem. Take social media , technology is supposed to make our life better. It must be net good in all the previously mentioned criteria, but we optimize for ad revenue, for user retention disregarding the user wellbeing and health.

Is your software as wholesome as ls?

Does it show directories and files without jeopardizing your attention span or wellbeing, without psychologically bombarding you with ads or stealing your data to maximize revenue?

Questions I can't stop asking

  • Why not optimize for wellbeing first and foremost, and keep profits positive , and that's it?
  • Why the greed, why the push for more?
  • Why is the experience and joy of computing & problem solving reduced to mere capitalist B2B utilities that maximize consumption and render the environment and end users worse off?

A different path

I propose a new initiative in my domain: altruistic, sustainability-positive, impact-driven development. Development that values sustainability, wellbeing, health, curiosity and altruism as first principles , with healthy, acceptable profits as a constraint, not the north star.

I'm not against commodities or business , but let's design systems, platforms and products whose primary optimization is for human and ecological flourishing, not for endless growth at any cost.

Values (short)

  • Sustainability
  • Wellbeing & health
  • Meaningful impact
  • Curiosity & learning
  • Altruism
  • Responsible profit (enough, not endless)
